Lunar Mission Rocket Design

Conceptual design of a two-stage launch vehicle to deliver a 50 kg payload to lunar orbit, including staging, trajectory, and propellant trade studies.

Overview

The project develops mission requirements into a sized vehicle: mass and Δv budgets link propulsion choices to orbital targets. Trajectory tools connect Earth departure to lunar capture assumptions using patched-conic and vis-viva methods where appropriate.

Technical work

  • Sized a two-stage stack to deliver a 50 kg payload to lunar orbit using the Tsiolkovsky rocket equation and staging logic.
  • Performed Δv budgeting, mass breakdown, and stage separation considerations.
  • Completed trajectory analysis using vis-viva and patched-conic approximations.
  • Evaluated LOX/RP-1 and NTO/MMH options with NASA CEA for performance context.
